Women of Color, Honor and Ambition (WOCHA)

WOCHA is a one-year program open to all women who are in their second- through fifth-years of study at the university, with an emphasis on women of color. The WOCHA program aims to create an inspiring vision of the future by enhancing leadership ability, building sisterhood, accessing professional mentorship and open networking opportunities.

In the WOCHA program, each participant is partnered with an external and internal mentor. The external mentor is one that is within or close to the field in which the participant would like to go into. The internal mentor provides an opportunity for each woman to have an additional layer of support to navigate RIT resources.

Internal and external partners come and present workshops that tie into WOCHA’s 5 Pillars:

- Leadership Development

- Healthy Lifestyles

- Financial Literacy

- Self-E (Self-Esteem and Self-Empowerment)

- Community Engagement

ALANA Collegiate Association (ACA)

AALANA = African-, Latin- and Native-American
ALANA = African-, Latin-, Asian- and Native-American

It is the mission of the ALANA Collegiate Association to improve the overall collegiate experience and promote academic excellence for ALANA students at the Rochester Institute of Technology through advocacy, social engagement, cultural enrichment and access to resources.

It is the vision of the ALANA Collegiate Association to see ALANA students enjoy a successful and culturally enriched collegiate experience and to increase racial diversity in the RIT community (students, faculty and staff).

The purpose of the ALANA Collegiate Association is:

- To serve as a liaison between the ALANA student population and RIT administration;

- To advocate for ALANA students and address the needs that are unique to the ALANA student experience;

- To provide access to and awareness of resources on campus to improve ALANA student academic performance;

- To inform ALANA students of financial, cultural, educational, political and employment opportunities;

- To contribute to the academic, leadership and social development of ALANA students;

- To encourage a sense of pride in ALANA culture on RIT’s campus and raise awareness to non-ALANA students of ALANA student experiences and culture; and

-To promote and support the recruitment and enrollment of ALANA students and recruitment and hiring of ALANA faculty and staff.

Secretary (2016-17)

Liaison: Unity House, Gospel Ensemble, Afroblaze

With the integration of the Asian American community (change from AALANA to ALANA), it was more crucial than ever to encourage collaboration and support across the various cultures included in ALANA. I aspired to be a bridge that would unite all these cultures through my service and leadership. A community is made stronger when its members are able to use their abilities to help each other grow. Communication between ACA and constituents would need to be more open and fluid, so that ACA could advocate effectively on their behalf.

Joining ACA gave the opportunity to represent the ALANA community in a much bigger capacity. We had direct access to Student Government and could advocate for our students. As Secretary, I had the opportunity to reach many more students with the resources available to help them succeed, or even to help them get more involved on campus.

Whenever there was a change in policy, or a petition that could affect our constituents, information was included in our newsletter to inform them and ask for their support during Senate meetings if necessary. This relationship between ACA and constituents encouraged students to bring before us issues that concerned them, either directly or indirectly through PawPrints tasks that would get assigned to us – an example being a request for RIT to hang the Black Lives Matter flag on campus to show solidarity. Details and results were featured in our weekly newsletters sent to all our subscribers, including higher offices in the RIT community.

Unity House

Unity House, a special interest house of the Rochester Institute of Technology geared towards promoting social and academic success of it's members and the greater community.

Founded in 1986, Unity House is an on-campus special interest house designed to provide students interested in ALANA culture with a nurturing environment that fosters an awareness of culturally diverse thought and history. Our floor focuses on the academic, cultural, personal, and social needs of its house members. 

Our floor provides a welcoming atmosphere that promotes diversity and cultural awareness through various social and academic events that incorporate the entire RIT community, collaborating with many of RIT's various clubs and organizations to promote overall growth among the community. Our motto, "One House, One Family, One Love", addresses the three key goals that we strive to provide for our members:

One House. As a diverse and unique community, we are united on all fronts. By educating the people in our community, as well as the ones outside, we ensure that everyone is well informed about the backgrounds of others which makes it easier for us to stand together.

One Love. As a SIH, we love to see all of our members succeed in different aspects of their lives. Academically, personally, or professionally, we are here to guide you through it all.

One Family. Unity House can serve as a "home away from home" for many students. The bonds that you form with other members will last more than a lifetime. You can always feel the love shared between UHouse members as well as an endless sea of people waiting to support you at every turn.

Essentially, Unity House seeks to become a comfortable place to call a home away from home.

RIT Gospel Ensemble

RIT Gospel Ensemble is a formally recognized organization under Student Government. Our student operated organization of about thirty members comes from a variety of backgrounds. We do not audition for our group, but rather allow God to work through us to divine His praise and worship. 

Our group welcomes RIT faculty, staff, students, and alumni, as well as members of the other colleges in the Rochester community. Our membership includes deaf, hard-of-hearing, and hearing individuals. We have several concerts and engagements throughout the year including Brick City Festival / Homecoming Weekend, Gospel Fest, and our anniversary. Often we participate in or host intercollegiate choir festivals. 

Through our ministry we have been able to bring major recording artists such as Hezekiah Walker, Richard Smallwood, Byron Cage, and most recently Jekalyn Carr to the RIT and Rochester community.

RIT National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE)

With more than 29,900 members, NSBE is one of the largest student-governed organizations in the country. That includes more than 394 College, Pre-College, and Technical Professional/Alumni chapters in the United States and abroad.

The National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE) is a 501(C)(3) non-profit association that is owned and managed by its members. The organization is dedicated to the academic and professional success of African-American engineering students and professionals. 

NSBE offers its members leadership training, professional development, mentoring opportunities, career placement services and more! NSBE is comprised of more than 250 collegiate, 68 professional and 99 pre-college active chapters nationwide and overseas.

NSBE is governed by an executive board of college students and engineering professionals and is operated by a professional staff in our World Headquarters located in Alexandria, VA.

NSBE with its unique characteristics, has accomplished more for Black engineering students than any other organization in the world. The same light that NSBE spreads to students and professionals in the United States is also relevant in African, European, South American, Asian, Caribbean, Canadian, Australian and Pacific Islander countries for people of color. It is the NSBE leadership's vision that the organization will replicate itself in countries around the world, creating a world network of Black engineers, scientists and technologists through its international operations.
